End of Life
  • In addition to being the Prophet of Islam in Medina, Muhammad was also the Head of State. In this role he had to regulate multi-religions. Consequently, Muhammad wrote “The Chart of Media” AKA laws that were established in Medina to unite all faiths. Muhammad recognized that “people of the book” jews, christians and all other religions had the right to practice their own religion freely without consequence

  • At first, the Meccans did not like the Prophets message and rejected it so much that they were planning to kill Muhammad

  • Even after Muhammad fled to Medina, Mecca still faced violent turbulence. Meccans were constantly attacking Muslims in Medina, yet despite this, the Muslims never started a fight first

  • This yet again highlights how much of a peaceful religion Islam is

  • 630 was the last battle and the Meccans FINALLY surrendered. Muhammad came to Kaaba, Mecca

  • While he was there, he gave amnesty to ALL Meccans Yet again, Muhammad proved to be diplomatic, compassionate and forgiving

  • After this event many Meccans joined Islam & became apart of the Muslim community

  • The war between Mecca and Medina was finally over – united over Islam

  • Muhammad eventually died in 632
