Major Beliefs/ Teachings in the Qur'an

Learn what the Qur'an is, what it's teachings are and how it's used as source for moral and ethical guide for Muslims.

  • Website
  • Twitter
  • Unsplash

The Qur'an is a religious text, similar to the Bible or Torrah, the Qur'an offers spiritual insight and guidence. The Qur'an mostly talks about ethics and morality – that is, how to be a good person and overall good Muslim.


Note: Any page reference you see is personally dervived from the Oxford translated version of the Qur'an. ISBN-10: 0199535957

Basic Facts:
  • The Qur’an is the literal word of God sent to Muhammad
  • It consists of 114 chapters and 6,000 versus
  • The Qur’an took about 22-23 years to write and receive these revelations
  • Its shorter in length to the New Testament
  • Chapters are arranged by length (big → small)
  • The first part of the book consists of revelations that were received by Muhammad while in Mecca
  • The last part of the book is more mystical and deals with ideas of death, life after death, etc.
  • The first chapters have 7 versus which are recited by Muslims everyday in prayer
  • The Qur’an confirms the torah + bible as being legitimate revelations from God, but that some human fabrications have been inserted (i.e. holy trinity)
  • Trinity (shared power) is not believed by Muslims, but they sure believe in Jesus as a highly valued prophet (ch. 19)
  • “Allah” is the one , all serving God
  • The Qur’an is seen as a correction of these other abrahamic faiths – NOT a fabrication
  • Allah is portrayed as transcendent, the judge of all of the universe in the Qur’an
  • Allah's character is revealed, his will is made clear
  • The relationship between Allah and his people is: Unconditional love, Intimate, and Compassionate & merciful
  • About 96% of the Qur’an is all about morality There is no original sin or disgrace, shame, etc.
  • Doing just is constantly emphasized
  • Promotes Muslim society based on unity, the day of judgment, and eternal life (after death)
