Lack of Education and the Start of Arab Spring

Lack of education in rural areas still exist today. The distorted teachings of Islam and the term Jihad divide Muslims and train the next generation to think radically, violently and militantly, thus lessening the chance for a true, working democracy in Muslim majority countries. In 2011, Muslims throughout the Middle East, Asia and Africa started to become aware of these false, Wahabi notions and it led to an “awakening” across Muslim majority countries. In the end however, the awakening did more harm than good. The movement divided Muslims more than before. The “Spring (or Arab) Awakening made its first rounds in Libya, Tunisia, Egypt and Syria. The Spring Awakening is one of the main causes for the uprising in Syria today. This politically driven event was yet again intertwined with religion and it divided the Sunnis and Shiites -- forcing them against each other due to the support/lack of support for the Assad regime. This division rooted in religion started an influx of opposition Sunni and Jihadist groups that infiltrated not just Syria, but other regions in South Asia and the Middle East. As per usual in Islamic countries, religion got the best of politics and the Arab spring led to the most growth ISIS has ever had, giving these terror organizations power and most of all expansion.

The politics of Muslim majority countries are complex, but it’s clear to see that the issues of terrorism have less to do with Islam and more with opposition/terror groups using the religion as a political tool.
