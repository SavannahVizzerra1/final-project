Background

Islam is an Abrahamic (monotheistic) religion. It stems from the same branch as Christianity and Judaism. All faiths believe in one God. In Islam, Muslims use the Arabic word “Allah” when speaking to or about God. Allah is simply the Arabic word for God. The difference between the three religions are that Muslims believe in Abraham, Hagar and Ishmael while Christians and Jews believe in Sarah, Isaac and Abraham. (Don’t worry too much about these names now – they be covered in depth later.)

Definitions

  • Islam can be defined as “submission to the will of God and the peace that comes with”, thus technically anyone can be considered a follower of Islam! In simpler terms, it just means devotion to God and the spiritual rewards that come when one devotes their life to God.
  • A Muslim is a person who submits to the will of Islam (a follower, believer, etc.).
  • Salam is a common Arabic greeting. It means peace be with you, similar to what Catholics say in mass.

Basic Facts

  • Muslims pray 5 times a day & face the direction of Mecca when doing so (N.E.)
  • Two major historical divisions of Muslims are Sunnis, which make up 85% of the Muslim population and Shii’s, which make up 15% of the world's Muslim population Both of these divisions are united by the belief in Allah, the Qur’an and the teachings of Muhammad
  • Islam developed in Central Arabia
  • There are more than 1.6 billion followers of Islam around the globe and it’s the second largest religion in the world
  • Islam is also the fastest growing religion
  • Only 13% of Muslims identify as Arabs
  • Islam came to the forefront for the first time in the West during the Iranian Revolution in 1979 and after 9/11 in 2001
  • Islam is often misconstrued because of media portrayal/politics and lack of knowledge
