5 Pillars

Dive deeper into the religious beliefs and standards that Muslims hold themselves accountable to.

What are the 5 pillars of Islam?

Similar to the 10 commandments or any other religious set of guidelines, the five pillars guide Muslims throughout the course of their lives. They are as follows:

1. Confession of faith (shaddah)
  • There is no god but god
  • Muhammad is the prophet
2. Prayer (salat)
  • Having god be present in your life at all times 5 prayer times: daybreak, noon, mid afternoon, sunset and late evening
  • Recommended to pray with others, Have to pray in the direction of Mecca (N.E.)
  • You are supposed to clean yourself before prayer – wash your hands, face, feet, etc. to cleanse spiritually...it gives you purity so you can focus on only God while praying
  • Jumma prayer – 12:00PM Friday prayer. Recommended to do at Mosque (Masjid) or Islamic Community Center
3. Alms giving (zakat)
  • Reminds all Muslims that wealth is a gift from god & that it should be shared with the less fortunate
  • Collect 2.5% of total assets and pay as “zakat” at the end of the year To help those in need, To “free slaves”, or those living in extreme poverty
  • If you don’t know who to gives $$ to, you can give it to an Imam and he will distribute it accordingly
4. Fast of Ramadan
  • 9th month of the lunar islamic calendar, Its when Muhammad received his first message from God
  • Fast from sundown to sunset
  • Refrain from water, food, sexual activities
  • Common to break fast with snack then have a nice meal
  • Before sunset=suhor
  • Objective of ramadan is not to give you hardship but to remind you of the people who can’t eat regularly
  • During this month you should recite or listen to the Qur’an as much as possible
  • If you’re a really good Muslim, you read + finish the Qur’an during the month of Ramadan
  • The 27th day is a holy day as that's the way that Muhammad received revelation aka EID to break the fast!
5. Pilgrimage (Haj) (7 days long)
    5 steps during Haj
  • Circumambulation
  • Safa + marwah
  • Stoning of satan
  • Plain of arafat
  • Feast of sacrifice
  • 12 month of lunar calendar, 3 months after Ramadan
  • If you’re able to then you should go at least once in your lifetime
  • Haj commemorates the life of Prophet Muhammad...its symbolized by wearing white to represent purity, unity and equality
  • Idea that this goes above wealth, social status, nationality, etc.
