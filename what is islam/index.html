Background

Islam is an Abrahamic (monotheistic) religion. It stems from the same branch as Christianity and Judaism. All faiths believe in one God. In Islam, Muslims use the Arabic word “Allah” when speaking to or about God. Allah is simply the Arabic word for God. The difference between the three religions are that Muslims believe in Abraham, Hagar and Ishmael while Christians and Jews believe in Sarah, Isaac and Abraham.
